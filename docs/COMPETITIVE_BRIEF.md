## Archy

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Funding | Series B ~$20M (TCV / Bessemer) |
| Scale | ~57 staff, ~300% YoY growth cited |
| Payments | $100M+ processed, 45 states |
| AI brand | "Archy Intelligence" — 5 agents marketed |

**Live today**

- **Insight** — reporting / analytics agent
- **Verify** — eligibility agent
- **Scribe** — clinical notes (launched May 2026)

**Still in development**

- **Revenue** — billing agent (marketing ahead of product)

**Go-to-market:** Full PMS **replacement** required. They led with clinical notes (Scribe), not billing depth.

**User weakness (our wedge):** Ledger and billing are the **#1 complaint** in user feedback — despite AI branding.

**Our advantage**

- Ship real billing-agent depth while their Revenue agent is still vapor
- Integrate-don't-replace on Dentrix base they cannot serve without migration
- Warm payer-intelligence moat from routed claims (they start cold on billing outcomes)

## Curve Dental

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Installed base | 80,000+ professionals |
| Migrations | 2,500–4,000+ cited |
| Funding | ~$20M/yr + $60M/3yr structure (public reporting) |
| Infra | AWS, decade-mature platform |

**AI surface**

- **FLO AI — Eligibility+** — code-level AI verification; their **strongest** billing AI today
- **Ask CurveAI** — general assistant
- **Partnership AI** — Mango, Bola, Pearl (stitched, not native depth)

**Strategic signal:** President Macon has stated they are **training on claims / denial data** — they see the data-moat thesis too.

**Roadmap emphasis:** Clinical AI expansion, not deeper standalone RCM vertical.

**User weakness (our wedge):** Billing and payments described as "confusing"; reporting weak; migration overhead and outage history cited in market chatter.

**Our advantage**

- Their outcome data is locked behind rip-and-replace and diluted across ~10 PMS modules
- Ours is warm-seeded + pure-play billing depth + **no migration**
- Execution on financial core before they deepen RCM

## Both incumbents — shared pattern

| Pattern | Implication for Backstop |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Rip-and-replace | Dentrix-heavy base **unserved** by both |
| Billing a top complaint **despite** AI marketing | Wedge is real, not hypothetical |
| Billing is ~1 of ~10 modules | We win vertical-deep on billing atop existing PMS |
| Years of capital on full PMS | Don't race on breadth — race on billing outcomes |

**Win path:** Integrate on Dentrix → dominate financial core → expand to all-in-one from billing trust + uncopyable moat.

---

## Risks to our thesis

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Archy funded + hiring AI aggressively | **Window closing** — move fast on billing depth + pilots |
| Curve publicly stated data-moat thesis | Edge = **execution + no-migration warm-start**, not the idea alone |
| Both have years/capital on full PMS | Sequence: billing dominance first; don't match module breadth early |
| Archy Revenue agent ships | Must have scrub + eligibility + moat **live** before their billing story catches up |
